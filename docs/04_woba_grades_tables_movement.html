4  wOBA Grades

4.1 Revisiting Jones and Tovar

At the time of writing this, Nolan Jones is right around 600 plate appearances for his early career with a wOBA between .360 and .370.

Ezequiel Tovar reached 600 career plate appearances last September with a .302 wOBA.

Jones was technically not a top 100 prospect, although he did rank as high as 45 on Baseball America’s (BA) 2021 preseason top 100 list. It’s probably fair to say he was a borderline top 100 prospect right around his debut, but based on our criteria, he falls into the Unranked prospect category.

Tovar ranked 17th on BA’s 2023 preseason list. His defensive skills contributed in a big way to that ranking, but he would not have been that high had his bat been a projected liability.

You can see where Jones (Above Average) and Tovar (Poor) fall based on FanGraphs’ wOBA ratings/grades. I’ve shortened “Above Average” to “Good” in the way I’ve labeled the grades.

4.2 A Rockie Riddle & a Kernel

So how did batters like Jones and Tovar (based on similar prospect rankings and initial career performances) do for the rest of their career?

You can flip through the tables below to find out.

As a guide for what you are looking at, take the first row (Elite) of the first table (Top 25 prospects). Only one batter as indicated in the Total column is represented here, so the 100% in the Avg. (Average) column means that this batter had an Elite wOBA at 600 plate appearances and an Average wOBA for the remainder of their career.

The batter? Austin Kearns!

4.4 Answering the Riddle

You probably figured it out! With regard to Jones, there were 105 unranked prospects with a wOBA grade of Good for their first 600 plate appearances. Only 2% got better, 17% remained Good, 30% declined to Average, 20% Below Average or Poor, and 30% Did Not Qualify, meaning that they did not last more than two more full seasons in the major leagues (approximately 1200 more plate appearances)—1800 career plate appearances.

Since Jones was a borderline top 100 prospect, it might make sense to also reference the table for prospects ranked 76 - 100.

Even if we look at those prospects, a higher percentage of them had a worse wOBA grade for the remainder of their career (58%). It’s important to note that Jones is at the higher end of the Good wOBA grade (.360 - .370 in a range from .370 to .340).

For Tovar, eleven top 25 prospects began their career with a Poor wOBA. Five of them (45%) improved to have a Good wOBA for the remainder of their career, four (36%) to an Average wOBA, and two (18%) remained Poor.

4.5 Gathering Round the Table(s)

While these tables are helpful to simply have as a reference, there are some macro-observations we can make.

The biggest is that contrasting outcomes for players similar to Jones and Tovar should give us enough pause when making conclusions early on in a player’s career, either good or bad, and drive home the importance of looking at a player’s prospect rank/scouting report.

Particularly if you look at the Total row at the bottom, it’s easy to see how higher ranked prospects have better career outcomes than lower ranked prospects.

Bad top ranked prospects are more likely to improve.
